"I think it's an artist's function to reflect his times." says Joe Herchel, guitarist for Gainesville rockers Hollow Days."The Doors had an album out in the late 60's called 'Strange Days' and I'm sure those were strange days to say the least. Then I thought about the music of our generation, with Britney, bling-bling and boy bands. There's no soul there. It's hollow. It's all about big titty bitches and dance steps. That's what we're against. We wanna bring back the emotion and meaning that seems to be missing in a lot of today's music. So that's why we called the band Hollow Days. It's a statement about our culture."

   Hollow Days is a hard rock band out of Gainesville whose powerful, high energy songs are making a strong impression on the Florida music scene. Instead of trying to follow the trends of the moment, the guys in Hollow Days do what they know best: straight up solid hard rock. Inspired and influenced by bands like Black Sabbath, Stone Temple Pilots and Guns n' Roses, Josh Mualdin (vocals), Joe Herchel (guitar), Jeremy Redding (bass, vocals), and Luke Pidgeon(drums), craft songs that are at once original yet somehow familiar. Strong hooks, soaring choruses and killer groves conduct the power and emotion of these songs to the listener.

    In the short time the band has been together, they have built a strong following primarily due to the strength of their live show. Josh Mauldin is a frontman in every sense of the word, commanding the stage and crowd with his high level of passion and energy. He has a great vocal range and incredible presence. The man was born to perform! Speaking of performing, guitarist Joe Herchel is a wild man on stage, often appearing possessed by the music as he effortlessly peels off blistering leads and bone crunching riffs. "Guitar Joe", as he is called by fans, eats, sleeps and breathes music. Because of his many talents, Jeremy Redding could be considered the glue of the group, holding the songs together with atmospheric  and creative bass playing. His voice blends with Josh's perfectly, adding a level of depth and texture most bands just don't have. If Jeremy is the glue, then Luke is definitely the backbone. His thunderous drum beats provide a rock solid foundation for the band. Blessed with an intuitive sense of dynamics and structure, his powerful drumming makes each song stand out. The chemistry these four have together is nothing short of amazing.

   Recently the band entered Rec Room Recording Studios and cut three of their most popular tunes. The resulting CD is an effective demonstration of the traits that helped make the band so popular. Since its release, stations such as Rock 104 103.7 FM, Real Rock 101.1 and The Buzz 100.5 FM have given the band airtime. Hollow Days has played in numerous cities throughout Florida, and are currently looking to expand their fan base the old fashioned way: getting up on stage and putting on a kick-ass rock show, winning the crowds over one person at a time. Though maintaining a website (www.hollowdays.com), handling out and posting fliers, sending out updates to their mailing list, contacting club owners, radio stations, other bands and various media outlets can be grueling, these guys actually enjoy these activities because they know that it will lead to yet another opportunity to step on stage, feel the lights, hear the crowd, and kick out the jams.
